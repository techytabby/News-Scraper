South Korea to close temporary embassy in Lviv citing ""escalating military threats From CNN's Gawon Bae in Seoul South Korea South Korea will close its temporary embassy in Lviv due to “escalating military threats” near the western Ukrainian city the Foreign Ministry said in a statement Friday. Heightening military threats near Lviv made it difficult for the temporary embassy “to function and to secure safety of its staff” the statement said. South Korean moved its embassy in Ukraine from the capital Kyiv to Lviv on March 3. It is also operating temporary offices in Chemivtsi southwestern Ukraine and in Romania which will remain open.
