Russia says there will be no domestic food shortages Russia has said there is no risk of a food shortage on the domestic market and cautioned consumers against rushing out to stock up on staples after the West slapped sanctions on Russia. “There are no conditions for risks of a shortage or for a reduction in product range” Deputy Prime Minister Viktoria Abramchenko said in a statement. “It is not worth driving up artificial demand with purchases for the future” Abramchenko said. “We will reorient the market and establish mutually profitable trade expand our partnership network with friendly countries.”
