Anguished Ukrainians await news of relatives in besieged Mariupol Roman Skliarov woke up on February 24 to a phone call warning that war had started. In the distance he could hear the sound of explosions and heavy fire as Russian troops advanced towards the port city of Mariupol in southeastern Ukraine. The 32-year-old rushed to wake his mother and younger brother who tried to calm him down as he flung a few essential items into a backpack. “Tt took me two hours to convince them to leave” Skliarov said.
