Australia imposes new sanctions on Russian banks billionaires Australia has placed sanctions on 11 additional Russian banks and government organisations covering the majority of the country’s banking assets along with all entities that handle Russia's sovereign debt. “With our recent inclusion of the Central Bank of Russia Australia has now targeted all Russian government entities responsible for issuing and managing Russia's sovereign debt” Minister for Foreign Affairs Marise Payne said in a statement. She said Australia is also placing sanctions on two more Russian billionaires with links to business interests in the country — Oleg Deripaska and Viktor Vekselberg.
