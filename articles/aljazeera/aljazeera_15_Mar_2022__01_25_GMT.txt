Chechen fighters ‘leading’ Russian assault on Mariupol The Kremlin-backed leader of the Russian region of Chechnya says that Chechen fighters are spearheading a Russian offensive on the strategic port of Mariupol. Chechnya’s regional leader Ramzan Kadyrov said on the messaging app Telegram that Chechen fighters went 1.5 kilometres (about 1 mile) inside the Sea of Azov city before pausing their attack when night fell. Kadyrov said his close associate Adam Delimkhanov is leading Chechen fighters in Mariupol.
