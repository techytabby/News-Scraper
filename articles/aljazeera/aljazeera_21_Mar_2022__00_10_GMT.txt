Ukraine rejects Russia's demand for Mariupol surrender Iryna Vereshchuk Ukraine’s deputy prime minister has rejected a Russian call for Ukrainian forces in the besieged city of Mariupol to lay down their arms saying “there can be no question of any surrender”. In comments to Ukrainska Pravda Vereshchuk said the Russian forces were demanding a surrender before allowing the evacuation of civilians. She described the demand as “real hostage-taking”.
