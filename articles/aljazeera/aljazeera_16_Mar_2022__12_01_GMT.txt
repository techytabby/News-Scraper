Kremlin says neutral Ukraine like Austria is possible compromise Russia has said that a neutral Ukraine with its own army along the lines of Austria or Sweden is being looked at as a possible compromise in peace talks with Kyiv. “This is a variant that is currently being discussed and which could really be seen a compromise” Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov was quoted as saying by RIA news agency. Peskov was commenting on remarks from Vladimir Medinsky Russia’s chief negotiator who earlier told state TV: “Ukraine is offering an Austrian or Swedish version of a neutral demilitarised state but at the same time a state with its own army and navy.” Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov talked on the continuing negotiations [File: Evgenia Novozhenina/Reuters]
