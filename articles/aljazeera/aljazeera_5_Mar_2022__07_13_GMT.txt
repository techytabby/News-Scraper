PayPal shuts down its services in Russia Payments company PayPal Holdings Inc has shut down its services in Russia citing “the current circumstances” joining many financial and tech companies in suspending operations there.  “Under the current circumstances we are suspending PayPal services in Russia” President and Chief Executive Dan Schulman said in a statement. He added that the company “stands with the international community in condemning Russia's violent military aggression in Ukraine”. A company spokesperson said PayPal will support withdrawals “for a period of time ensuring that account balances are dispersed in line with applicable laws and regulations”.
