Russia aims to stop Ukraine from joining NATO Lavrov says Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov has said Moscow’s attack on Ukraine is aimed among other things at ensuring that its neighbour does not join the NATO transatlantic military alliance. Lavrov told Russian state television that the country’s forces were attacking military targets but added that the term “collateral damage” has been in use since military campaigns waged by Washington and its Western allies in Iraq and Libya. He added that Western powers were aware of Moscow's concerns and said they would have to address them at some point before referring to sanctions imposed on Russia over its invasion as a “tax on independence”.
