World Bank halts all programmes in Russia Belarus The World Bank says it has stopped all programmes in Russia and Belarus with immediate effect following the Russian invasion and “hostilities against the people of Ukraine”. Ina statement the World Bank said it had not approved any new loans to or investments in Russia since 2014 the year Russia annexed Ukraine's Crimean Peninsula. The bank said it had not approved any new lending to Belarus since mid-2020 when the US imposed sanctions on the country over a disputed presidential election.
