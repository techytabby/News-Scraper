Ukraine says civilians unable to leave Mariupol; Zelenskyy blames Russian ‘terror’ Not a single civilian was able to leave the encircled Ukrainian city of Mariupol as Russian forces failed to respect a temporary ceasefire to allow evacuations Ukraine’s deputy prime minister has said. Meanwhile efforts to send food water and medicine into the city failed when Russian tanks attacked a humanitarian corridor President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said. “This is outright terror ... from experienced terrorists” he said. “The world needs to know this. I have to admit it — we are all dealing with a terrorist state.”
