Situation in Mariupol ‘very difficult’ Ukrainian official says Ukraine’s deputy prime minister has described the situation in Mariupol as “very difficult” after Kyiv rejected a Russian ultimatum to surrender the city. “Of course we rejected these proposals” Iryna Vereshchuk said. “The situation there is very difficult.” Moscow had offered to open humanitarian corridors for evacuations from Mariupol from 10am Moscow time (07:00 GMT) had the surrender proposal been accepted.
