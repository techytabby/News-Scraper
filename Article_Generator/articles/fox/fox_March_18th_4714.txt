President Biden and Chinese President Xi Jinping spoke on a secure video call for nearly two hours Friday morning discussing Russia's war against Ukraine and “other issues of mutual concern.” Biden took the call which started at 9:03 a.m. ET in the White House Situation Room. The call concluded at 10:53 a.m. ET. The White House previewed the call as part of the administration's “ongoing efforts to maintain open lines of communication’ between the United States and the People's Republic of China (PRC).
