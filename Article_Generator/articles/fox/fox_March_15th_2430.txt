2000 civilian cars have escaped Mariupol: report Satelite mages show burning and destroyed apartment buildings Monday in westem Mariupol Ukraine. (Maxar Technologies/Getty images) Mariupol's city council said Tuesday that around 2000 civilian cars have managed to leave the besieged Ukrainian port city through a so- called humanitarian corridor leading west. The council said another 2000 cars are in the city but waiting to leave along the route which runs for more than 160 miles to the Ukraine- held city of Zaporizhzhia. Kyiv mayor Vitali Klitschko said on social media the corridor would be open from 8 p.m. local time Tuesday to 7 a.m. Thursday. The Associated Press contributed to this report.
