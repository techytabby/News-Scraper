UN says 331 civilians killed amid Russia's offensive The UN’s human rights office (OHCHR) says it has confirmed that 331 civilians including 19 children have been killed in Ukraine since Russia’s invasion began on February 24. OHCHR which has monitors in Ukraine added that 675 others had been wounded as of Thursday midnight and warned the true toll from the conflict was likely much higher. Most of the victims were killed by explosive weapons such as shelling from heavy artillery multi-launch rocket systems and missile and air raids it said. Al Jazeera was unable to independently verify the figures provided.
