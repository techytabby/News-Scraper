More than 3500 detained at anti-war protests in Russia More than 3500 people have been detained at protests across Russia against President Vladimir Putin’s invasion of Ukraine according to data provided by Russian authorities. Russia's interior ministry said 1700 people had been detained in Moscow 750 in St Petersburg and 1061 in other cities. The OVD-Info protest monitoring group said it had documented the detention of at least 2578 people in 49 different cities.
