Ukraine's emergency service says 22 killed in Chernihiv air raids Ukraine’s emergency service says at least 22 people have been killed in Russian air raids in Ukraine’s northern Chernihiv region. The service said in a post on Twitter that rescue work was ongoing. It did not specify where the alleged attack took place. Earlier the regional governor said at least nine people had been killed in an air raid on two schools and private houses. Al Jazeera was unable to independently verify either claim.
