US does not believe Russian assault on Odesa is imminent: Official The US does not believe a Russian amphibious assault in or near the Ukrainian city of Odesa is imminent a senior US defence official has said amid growing concern about a potential attack on the city. The official who spoke to the Reuters news agency on condition of anonymity estimated Russia had launched about 600 missiles since the start of its invasion and deployed into Ukraine about 95 percent of combat forces it had pre-staged outside of the country. Russian forces continued to try to advance and isolate Kyiv Kharkiv and Chernihiv and are meeting “strong Ukrainian resistance” the official said.
