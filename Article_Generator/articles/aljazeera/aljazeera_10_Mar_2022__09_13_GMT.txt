Kremlin says it will seek information from military about hospital bombing Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov says Moscow will seek information from the Russian military over its alleged bombing of a children’s hospital in Mariupol. “We will certainly ask our military about this since we don’t have clear information about what happened there. And the military are very likely to provide some information” Peskov told reporters at a news briefing. Other Russian officials took a more aggressive line however rejecting the reported strike as fake news. “This is information terrorism” foreign ministry spokeswoman Maria Zakharova said.
